On the contrary, it appears that something which fulfills its final end is better than something that does not.

Take for example a cup. A cup's final end, or nature, is to hold something, most likely a drink. If a cup has a hole in the bottom, or a crack on the side, it is considered to be a bad cup. The reason for this being that the cup can no longer effectively hold a drink in it. Therefore, we say that the cup is worse because it did not fulfill its final end.

Therefore, it would appear that something which fulfills its final end is better than something which does not.
